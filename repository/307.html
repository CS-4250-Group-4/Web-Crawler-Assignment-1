    Freshman Checklist

    The items on this checklist will prepare you for your first semester at Cal Poly Pomona. You can track your checklist progress in the CPP Online Orientation.

    After Accepting Your Admission Offer (May)

    Orientation reservations will be available from March 15 to May 9. Making your reservation signs you up for both the CPP Online Orientation and the in-person, on-campus Orientation Day that you select. To make your reservation:

    • Log in to MyCPP.
    • Click on Reserve Your Spot in Orientation.
    • Follow the prompts to confirm your reservation.
    You will receive a confirmation email when your reservation is confirmed. You must make your orientation reservation by 11:59 p.m. (Pacific Time) on May 9, otherwise your admission to CPP will be withdrawn.

    All students accessing the campus and university programs are required to be up to date with their COVID-19 vaccines and upload their vaccination record for verification. You must upload your COVID-19 vaccination record no later than 2 weeks prior to your Orientation Day. Please note you will be able to upload your vaccination record the next day after accepting your admission offer.

    Students requesting a religious or medical exemption can find the forms on the Safer Return website. Religious or medical exemption requests must be submitted no later than 2 weeks prior to your Orientation Day.

    If you have guests attending orientation with you, more information about COVID-19 health and safety guidelines for guests will be included in the reminder emails sent closer to your orientation date.

    The link to the English Directed Self-Placement (DSP) Survey will be sent to your CPP email address later this spring. You can search for the subject line Required: CPP Directed Self-Placement (DSP) Survey. The survey takes about 10 minutes to complete.

    More information on the English Directed Self-Placement (DSP) Survey

    In general, the following majors need to take the ALEKS PPL Assessment unless exempt from it:

    • College of Science: All majors except Kinesiology.
    • College of Engineering: All majors.
    • College of Agriculture: Food Science and Technology, Nutrition Science, and Nutrition and Health majors.

    More specifically, if your major requires you to take MAT 1140, MAT 1200, or MAT 1300, then you are required to take the ALEKS PPL assessment unless you have achieved one of the following:

    • You earned a 3 or higher on the AP AB and/or BC Calculus exam.
    • You took College Algebra (as the prerequisite for MAT 1200) or College Algebra and Trigonometry (as the prerequisites for MAT 1140 and MAT 1300) at an accredited college and earned a C or higher.
    • You earned a 4 or higher on IB Mathematics HL.

    NOTE: Your SAT math score and/or your ACT math score do not make you eligible to take MAT 1140, MAT 1200, or MAT 1300, regardless of your scores. You are only exempt from taking the ALEKS PPL assessment if you meet the conditions stated above.

    You will not be able to register for a mathematics course without taking the ALEKS PPL Assessment. Complete ALEKS prior to your in-person Orientation Day. Since ALEKS is designed to be a guided learning experience, start your work on ALEKS no later than 2 weeks before your in-person Orientation Day. This will give you time to complete the required learning modules in order to do your best on the official fourth assessment (and optional fifth assessment). You will spend about 15 hours in total on ALEKS during the summer.

    If you took the AP or IB exams in spring 2022, please also take the ALEKS PPL Assessment. You likely will not have your scores by orientation.

    Part 1 of the CPP Online Orientation will be available beginning May 15. You must finish Parts 1 and 2 prior to your in-person Orientation Day in order to register for classes. Part 1 provides an introduction to CPP as well an overview of important resources and services you need to know to prepare for your in-person Orientation.

    All videos in the CPP Online Orientation are closed captioned. If you would like to have the CPP Online Orientation text read aloud, we recommend viewing the orientation using the Chrome browser with the Read Aloud extension enabled.

    The Bronco Card is the official Cal Poly Pomona photo identification card for all Cal Poly Pomona faculty, staff, students and affiliates. It can also be used for dining services and convenience store locations, as well as access to campus services, buildings, and laboratories.

    In order to receive your Bronco Card at your in-person Orientation Day:

    1. Visit the Bronco Card website.
    2. Review the requirements.
    3. Follow the instructions to upload your photo AND government-issued Photo ID (e.g., driver’s license, passport, military ID, etc.).

    Before Orientation Day (June)

    Part 2 of the CPP Online Orientation will be available beginning June 1. You must finish Parts 1 and 2 prior to your in-person Orientation Day in order to register for classes. Part 2 covers important information about academic advising and registration to prepare you for your in-person Orientation day.

    All videos in the CPP Online Orientation are closed captioned. If you would like to have the CPP Online Orientation text read aloud, we recommend viewing the orientation using the Chrome browser with the Read Aloud extension enabled.

    For details about submitting official transcripts and test scores, visit the Admitted Freshmen section of the CPP Admission website.

    All incoming new students must create their Plan on CPP Connect's Planner BEFORE their Orientation Day. Having your Plan ready before Orientation Day will help ensure that you are well-prepared for advising and registration. You will receive access to Planner in early summer, prior to your Orientation Day. CPP Connect's Planner allows you to:

    • View the degree requirements for your primary major 
    • Plan out your coursework, term by term 
    • Share your Planner with your college advisor for recommendations 
    • Keep track of your completed major course requirements alongside your curriculum sheet and Degree Progress Report

    If you need services through the Disability Resource Center, it's important to apply early to ensure your accommodations are in place by the time the semester starts.

    Disability Resource Center Registration Process

    Incoming military-affiliated or veteran students should plan on attending a VRC Orientation Workshop hosted by the Veteran's Resource Center, prior to their in-person Orientation Day. During this session, you will learn about the services offered by the Veterans Resource Center and receive documents which will be needed during orientation in order to begin utilizing VA Educational Benefits. This informational workshop is open to all incoming veteran and military dependent students and is held in addition to Orientation Day.

    When selecting your VRC workshop date, please refer to the email you received from the Veterans Resource Center to determine which VRC workshop you should attend. Attendance for this workshop should be one week PRIOR to your in-person Orientation Day.

    RSVP for a VRC Pre-Orientation Workshop

    For questions about the VRC workshop, or to schedule a time to meet outside of your scheduled workshop, please contact the Veterans Resource Center at 909-869-6994 or vetquestions@cpp.edu.

    In addition to the orientation enrollment requirement, international students must also complete their Onboarding Checklist to avoid any registration holds. For more information about the Onboarding Checklist and studying at CPP as an international student, visit the International Students and Scholars Office website.

    Go to the Onboarding Checklist

    Before your in-person Orientation Day, be sure to review the New Student Registration Guide and check out Student Success Central for key advising resources, the academic calendar, videos, contact information, and more.

    In order to register for your first-semester classes at orientation, you must clear all negative holds indicated in your BroncoDirect Student Center holds section. Some holds are simply there to provide information.

    Having up-to-date information in BroncoDirect ensures that the campus can contact you if needed. Plus you'll be able to use BillyChat! Log in to MyCPP to access BroncoDirect.

    Visit the BillyChat website for a tutorial on how to update your contact information in BroncoDirect.

    All incoming students can take the StrengthsQuest Assessment for FREE!

    The StrengthsQuest assessment gives you the opportunity to develop strengths by building on your greatest talents - the way in which you most naturally think, feel, and behave as a unique individual. With StrengthsQuest, you discover your greatest natural talents, improve your grades, and increase your learning by making the most of your talents and strategically determining a rewarding career path based on who you are as uniquely talented individual.

    Complete your FREE StrengthsQuest Assessment by following these instructions:

    1. Click the StrengthsQuest Assessment button below.
    2. Log in with your BroncoName and password.
    3. Click on Take the CliftonStrengths assessment option.
    4. It takes approximately 35 minutes to complete this assessment, be sure to set aside sufficient time.

    During Orientation Day (June-July)

    Attend, participate, and enjoy your Orientation Day! More information about Orientation Day will be included in your reminder emails that are sent to both your CPP and personal email addresses. Read these emails carefully and keep them for future reference.

    Orientation is a full day program. Plan to be at CPP for the entire day.

    If you do not attend your reserved Orientation Day, your admission to CPP will be withdrawn. If you wish to be considered for a reinstatement after being withdrawn, you must file an appeal with the Office of Admissions.

    If you can no longer attend your reserved Orientation Day, contact the Office of New Student Programs & Family Engagement AS SOON AS POSSIBLE to reschedule. Be certain to include your Bronco ID number so we can assist you quickly.

    During advising and registration, you will meet in a small group with your advisor and other students in your major and then register for your first-semester classes. Before you leave advising and registration, make sure you are registered for 12-15 units, have your Plan complete, and know how to contact your advisor!

    All videos in the CPP Online Orientation are closed captioned. If you would like to have the CPP Online Orientation text read aloud, we recommend viewing the orientation using the Chrome browser with the Read Aloud extension enabled.
