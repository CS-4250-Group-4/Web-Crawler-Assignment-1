    Understanding Your Course Instruction Modes: Spring 2022 and Future Terms

    Courses at Cal Poly Pomona are offered in a variety of formats called instruction modes. It's important that you understand the instruction mode for your courses so you know what to expect. 

    Step 1: Finding the Instruction Mode

    Click on the Class or Section hyperlink for the section you’re interested in. 

    Click on your Student Center’s weekly schedule. Toggle to the List View, and then click on the hyperlinked section number.

    Face-to-Face Mode

    Face-to-Face

    A class that meets face-to-face on campus and/or off-campus. You are required to meet during the specified day(s) and time(s), if any. Your syllabus, grades, and class materials may be posted online.

    Online Modes

    Fully Synchronous

    A class that is instructed fully online at specified day(s) and time(s). You are not required to be on campus.

    Fully Asynchronous

    A fully online class that allows you to view instructional materials at any time. There won’t be specific days and times for you to log in, so you should build time for the class into your schedule.

    Bisynchronous

    New for Spring 2022: Previously Known as Fully Synchronous with TBA

    A class that is instructed fully online with both specified meeting day(s)/ time(s) and TBA meetings. You are required to meet online during the specified days(s) and time(s), with the rest of the class instruction delivered asynchronously.

    Hybrid Modes

    Hybrid Synchronous

    You are required to meet during the specified day(s) and time(s). Some meetings will be on campus and some will be online.

    Hybrid Asynchronous

    You are required to meet on campus during the specified day(s) and time(s), with the rest of the class instruction delivered asynchronously.

    HyFlex

    New for Spring 2022: Previously Categorized with Hybrid Synchronous

    For each meeting you will have the option to attend face-to-face, synchronously, or asynchronously.
