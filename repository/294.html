    Ceremony Changes FAQ

    To confirm your participation at commencement, all graduates must RSVP in addition to applying for graduation. The RSVP process will ensure that you will receive an official Grad Pass (in the form of a QR code) so you can enter event and will be used to cross the stage at ceremony.

    • March 24: You will receive an email from Marching Order (the RSVP system) asking you to confirm your participation.
    • April 21: Deadline for all graduates to RSVP via Marching Order.

    To manage the number of people coming to campus, all guests will need to show a valid ticket. Children under 2 years of age are free.  Every graduate will be able to claim eight tickets initially, and more tickets will be available later. All tickets will be free of charge.

    • March 24 - April 21: In the initial release, all graduates will receive an email to claim 8 tickets. After April 21, unclaimed tickets may be distributed to other students.
    • Late April: If possible, additional tickets will be released for graduates who request them. The amount to be determined by availability.

    The four larger ceremonies will be divided by department and major, with the goal of splitting the ceremonies evenly. Graduates will be assigned to one of the two ceremonies based on their major. 

    At this point, the planned maximum capacity for each commencement ceremony will not exceed 8,500 guests and graduates.

    All faculty is invited to attend commencement 2022, and will need to confirm their participation in advance with their college commencement liaison (representative).  Please note deadline for faculty regalia rentals/purchase is March 18th at the bookstore to ensure arrival prior to event without expedite fees. For additional questions and information, email commencement@cpp.edu.

    Visit the website for bookstore regalia orders. 

    For safety reasons, all bags are subject to search. All guests and graduates must go through the Bag Check areas around the Commencement venue. Visit the Commencement website for a list of prohibited items.

    Parking is free for all Commencement guests during Commencement weekend.

    • Graduate parking: Lot F
    • Guest Parking: Lot F, Parking Structure 1 and 2
    • ADA Parking: Lot C

    Cal Poly Pomona is located in Los Angeles County, and our Commencement ceremonies will follow the most current county and state guidelines.

    Currently, the protocols include:

    • Face masks or face coverings are not required for outdoor mega events. Voluntary wearing of masks is recommended (subject to change based on COVID-19 conditions)
    • Proof of vaccination will not be required of attendees. However, we strongly recommend everyone to be up to date with their vaccines (including getting the booster shot), as it is the best way to protect yourself and our community from COVID-19.

    Cal Poly Pomona will continue to follow public health and safety guidelines and restrictions, including that of the Los Angeles County Public Health Department and California Department of Public Health.
